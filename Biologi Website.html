Our Purpose

Borneo faces a stark reality: it has lost over 30% of its pristine forests since 1973, with projections indicating a further loss of 74,419 km2 by 2032‚Äîa significant 32% decline since the turn of the millennium. This degradation, fueled by agricultural expansion, industrial plantations, mining activities, and rampant forest fires, poses a grave threat to the island's unparalleled biodiversity.

In response to this environmental crisis, a native of Borneo felt compelled to act, spurred by a deep-rooted connection to the island's natural splendor. What began as a personal quest to document and preserve Borneo's ecological treasures evolved into a collective endeavor. Together, they launched a multifaceted platform dedicated to immortalizing and showcasing the region's beauty, raising awareness about conservation issues, and advocating for the protection of Borneo's invaluable ecosystems.
